## Design Context

### Users
B2B salespeople (high-earning reps doing 10-15 discovery calls/month on Google Meet). They use Sales-Wise during live calls — meaning they're multitasking, under pressure, and need information at a glance with zero friction. The job to be done: get the right qualification question whispered at exactly the right moment so they can close better.

### Brand Personality
**Calm, Smart, Effortless.** Sales-Wise is the quietly intelligent co-pilot that handles complexity so the rep doesn't have to. It earns trust by being invisible when not needed and precise when it is. No showboating, no noise — just the right insight at the right time.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Industrial-utilitarian meets polished productivity tool. Dark-mode-first. Mission control, not marketing.
- **References**: Raycast + Arc — polished but utilitarian, smart defaults, feels alive without being flashy.
- **Anti-references**: NO generic SaaS landing pages (purple gradients, floating mockups, "AI-powered" badges). NO Salesforce/HubSpot bloat (too many tabs, too much chrome). NO consumer chat apps (bubbly, casual, emoji-heavy).
- **Theme**: Dark mode primary. Cool gray neutrals with blue undertone. Indigo for interactive elements, orange for live/urgent states.

### Design Principles
1. **Invisible until needed** — The UI should disappear during a call. Zero visual noise. The live sidebar shows only what matters right now.
2. **Scannable in 2 seconds** — Every screen must communicate its primary message at a glance. Sales reps don't read — they scan between sentences.
3. **Reduced motion always** — Reps are on live calls. No animated entrances, no bouncing indicators, no attention-stealing transitions. Only micro-transitions (hover states, focus rings) at 50-100ms.
4. **Data speaks, decoration listens** — Typography, spacing, and color do the hierarchy work. No gradients, no glow effects, no decorative elements competing with actual content.
5. **Earn trust through consistency** — Same patterns everywhere. Same spacing. Same interaction model. If a rep learns one screen, every other screen should feel familiar.

### Typography
- **Display/Hero**: Satoshi (900, 700) — geometric, reads "designed" without being decorative
- **Body**: Plus Jakarta Sans (300-700) — geometric, friendly, excellent at small sizes
- **Data/Mono**: Geist Mono (400, 500) — tabular-nums for metrics, transcripts, timestamps

### Color System
- **Accent**: Indigo `#6366f1` (CTAs, links, selections)
- **Signal**: Orange `#f97316` (live state, recording, urgent)
- **Background**: `#0a0a0f` → `#12121a` → `#1a1a25` (depth layers)
- **Text**: `#e8e8f0` (primary), `#8b8ba0` (labels), `#6b6b80` (muted)
- **Semantic**: Green `#22c55e`, Yellow `#f59e0b`, Red `#ef4444`, Blue `#3b82f6`

### Accessibility
- WCAG AA minimum for all text contrast
- Reduced motion priority — sales reps on calls need zero distraction
- Keyboard navigable (power users expect it)
- No animation duration > 100ms except page transitions (250ms max)
